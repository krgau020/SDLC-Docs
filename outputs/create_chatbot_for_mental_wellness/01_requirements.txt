## **Original Client Requirements**

## **Refined Functional Requirements**

**1. Overall Goals & Objectives:**

*   **FR1.1 – Core Functionality:** The chatbot shall provide 24/7 access to mental wellness support and resources.
*   **FR1.2 – User Support:** The chatbot shall be designed to be user-friendly and accessible to individuals with varying levels of technical proficiency.
*   **FR1.3 – Empathy & Tone:** The chatbot shall communicate in a friendly, empathetic, and non-judgmental tone.
*   **FR1.4 – Privacy & Security:** The chatbot shall adhere to all relevant data privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA – depending on target audience and location) and implement robust security measures to protect user data. Data encryption both in transit and at rest is required.
*   **FR1.5 – Effectiveness Measurement:** The system shall incorporate mechanisms to track and measure the chatbot’s effectiveness in supporting user mental wellness (e.g., user satisfaction surveys, session duration, resource utilization).

**2. User Interaction & Conversation Flow:**

*   **FR2.1 – Initial Greeting:** The chatbot shall initiate a conversation with a welcoming and informative greeting, clearly stating its purpose and capabilities.
*   **FR2.2 – Topic Identification:** The chatbot shall be able to identify the user’s current emotional state and topic of concern through natural language processing (NLP).  This includes recognizing keywords, sentiment analysis, and potentially, topic modeling.
*   **FR2.3 – Open-Ended Questions:** The chatbot shall utilize open-ended questions to encourage users to elaborate on their feelings and experiences.
*   **FR2.4 – Response Variety:** The chatbot shall provide a diverse range of responses, avoiding repetitive or robotic phrasing.  Responses should be tailored to the user’s input.
*   **FR2.5 – Clarification Requests:** If the chatbot’s understanding of the user’s needs is unclear, it shall proactively request clarification.
*   **FR2.6 – Escalation Protocol:** The chatbot shall have a clearly defined escalation protocol to transfer the user to a human mental health professional or crisis hotline when:
    *   The user expresses suicidal ideation or intent.
    *   The user exhibits signs of severe distress or a mental health crisis.
    *   The chatbot is unable to adequately address the user’s needs.

**3. Content & Information Provision:**

*   **FR3.1 – Mental Health Information:** The chatbot shall provide accurate and evidence-based information about various mental health conditions (e.g., anxiety, depression, stress, PTSD).
*   **FR3.2 – Coping Strategies:** The chatbot shall offer a range of coping strategies and techniques (e.g., mindfulness exercises, relaxation techniques, cognitive reframing) based on the user’s needs.
*   **FR3.3 – Resource Directory:** The chatbot shall maintain a directory of relevant mental health resources, including:
    *   Crisis hotlines (e.g., Suicide Prevention Lifeline, Crisis Text Line).
    *   Mental health organizations (e.g., NAMI, Mental Health America).
    *   Therapist directories.
*   **FR3.4 – Personalized Recommendations:** Based on user input and tracked mood data (with user consent), the chatbot shall provide personalized recommendations for resources and coping strategies.

**4. Mood Tracking & Analysis (Optional - Phase 2):**

*   **FR4.1 – Mood Logging:** The chatbot shall allow users to log their mood on a regular basis (e.g., daily, weekly).
*   **FR4.2 – Mood Visualization:** The chatbot shall provide a visual representation of the user’s mood trends over time.
*   **FR4.3 – Pattern Identification:** The chatbot shall identify potential patterns in the user’s mood and provide insights (with user consent).

**5. Technical Requirements:**

*   **FR5.1 – Platform Compatibility:** The chatbot shall be accessible via multiple platforms (e.g., web, mobile app, messaging platforms – e.g., Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp).
*   **FR5.2 – NLP Engine:** The chatbot shall utilize a robust NLP engine for natural language understanding and generation.
*   **FR5.3 – Database:** The chatbot shall utilize a secure database to store user data (with appropriate encryption and access controls).
*   **FR5.4 – API Integrations:** The chatbot shall integrate with relevant APIs for accessing mental health resources and data.

**6. Testing & Quality Assurance:**

*   **FR6.1 – Unit Testing:** Individual components of the chatbot shall undergo thorough unit testing.
*   **FR6.2 – Integration Testing:** The integration of different components shall be tested to ensure seamless functionality.
*   **FR6.3 – User Acceptance Testing (UAT):** The chatbot shall be tested by a representative group of users to ensure it meets their needs and expectations.
*   **FR6.4 – Security Testing:** Regular security testing shall be conducted to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.
